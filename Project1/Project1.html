From 2009 to Present Day

2009

In 2009 any normal computer CPU could have mined bitcoin at a profit of $5 USD a day. Only a few people were mining bitcoin at this time and it still wasnâ€™t used as a viable source of commerce. Nobody knew the impact Bitcoin would end up having.

2010

In October of 2010 the code for mining bitcoin was released to the general public. This started the era of hobbiests and crypto nerds. Miners began adding GPU's (Graphic Processing Units) to their hardware. GPU's allowed for greater processing capabilities because they hash at a higher rate than CPU's. GPU's worked nearly 50-100 times faster than the average CPU. This introduction increased mining difficulty, in turn driving the need to improve hardware. The increase in hardware created an increase in energy consumption.

2011-2012

In June of 2011 a new kind of hardware revolutionized bitcoin mining. The FPGA (Field Programmable Gate Arrays) became the new rage in bitcoin mining. Hobbyiests could be tweaked to be useful for bitcoin mining. FPGA's were extremeley popular because they consumed about 3 times less power than the average GPU.

2013-Current

In 2013 the new and current standard to bitcoin techonology was introduced in the form of ASIC's. ASIC's have been around since the 1980's to increase graphic capabilities, but in 2013 they were created specifically for the purpose of bitcoin mining. ASIC unites increased the spead of algorithm soving by quite a lot. It has been estimated that ASIC's work 100,000 times faster than the most powerful CPU at calculating hashes. ASIC units are extremeley energy consuming and expensive. They brought bitcoin mining from hobby to industry.
